Post-quantum TLS is tempting to discuss as an algorithmic migration. In practice,
the interesting surface is wider: handshake size, certificate path shape, server
configuration, fallback behavior, middleboxes, and client support.

The useful question covers the deployment path: endpoint support, negotiation
behavior, chain shape, fallback, and traces that support each observation.

## What to measure

- Handshake size and round-trip behavior
- Certificate chain composition
- Extension negotiation and fallback paths
- Interoperability across clients and libraries
- Failure modes that appear only under realistic network conditions

## Measurement record

Measurement work becomes valuable when it is repeatable. A static report, a trace,
and a small explanation give the reader enough evidence to inspect the result.
