DNSSEC shows cryptographic migration as protocol behavior in resolvers, packets,
retries, and operational defaults.

When signatures and keys grow, the effects appear in packet size, truncation,
fragmentation, resolver choices, retries, and operational defaults. These are
systems behaviors, not footnotes.

## Boundary tests

The useful testbed changes one dimension at a time and records how the system
responds. The result is repeatable and easy to compare.

The artifact is the testbed: controlled input changes, captured responses, and
comparable reports.
