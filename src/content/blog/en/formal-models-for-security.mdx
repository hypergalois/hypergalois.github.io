Formalization turns a broad security statement into state, operations, invariants,
and obligations. The model makes the claim smaller and gives reviewers concrete
objects to inspect.

A useful model names the state, the operations, the invariants, and the conditions
under which the argument stops applying. That boundary is often the most valuable
part of the work.

## A practical standard

- Can the state be inspected?
- Are transitions explicit?
- Is the invariant doing real work?
- Can a reviewer find the assumptions quickly?
- Does the model connect back to the system it describes?

Formal methods clarify engineering judgment by making assumptions and transitions
explicit.
