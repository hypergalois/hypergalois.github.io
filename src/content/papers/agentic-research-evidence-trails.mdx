## Abstract

Automated research workflows need reviewable evidence: scenario inputs, tool
calls, intermediate decisions, touched files, failure labels, recovery behavior,
and final outputs. This manuscript outline treats agent runs as experimental
records that support comparison across systems and prompts.

## Methodology

The evaluation record stores the initial task, tool calls, touched files,
intermediate outputs, failure labels, recovery actions, and final diff. Runs are
compared at the scenario level, with enough structure to inspect both successful
and failed attempts.

## Results

The manuscript defines an evidence trail for agentic research workflows: a run
can be audited, replayed at the level of decisions, and compared across prompts,
models, and tool environments.
